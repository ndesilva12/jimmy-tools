✅ Background Check Checklist

DIY research using free public records — save $100+ per search

Step 1: Verify Identity

  • Confirm full legal name (including middle name)
  • Get date of birth if possible
  • Search FastPeopleSearch.com or TruePeopleSearch.com
  • Note all addresses from the past 7 years
  • Identify the county for each address

Step 2: Criminal Records (Search Each County)

  • Google "[County Name] court records search"
  • Search criminal case index by name + DOB
  • Note case numbers, charges, and dispositions
  • Repeat for EACH county where they've lived
  • Search PACER for federal cases (pacer.uscourts.gov)

Step 3: Sex Offender Registry

  • Search NSOPW.gov (national database)
  • Also check state-specific registry for more details

Step 4: Civil Records

  • Search county civil court for lawsuits
  • Check for eviction cases (may be in civil or small claims)
  • Search PACER for federal civil + bankruptcy
  • Note if they were plaintiff or defendant

Step 5: Employment/Education

  • Verify employer exists (Google search)
  • Check LinkedIn for work history
  • Verify degree: contact school registrar
  • Check accreditation: ope.ed.gov

Step 6: Professional Licenses

  • Search state licensing board
  • Verify license is active
  • Check for disciplinary actions
  • Medical: docinfo.org
  • Finance: brokercheck.finra.org

Step 7: Social Media Review

  • Search name on Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram, X
  • Check Namechk.com for username across platforms
  • Review recent posts for red flags
  • Google their email address and phone number
⚠️ Important: For employment or housing decisions, you may need to use an FCRA-compliant service and get written consent. This checklist is for personal/informal research only.
