Chapter 1: Introduction to OSINT

Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) is the collection and analysis of information from publicly available sources. In an age where people voluntarily share vast amounts of personal information online, OSINT has become one of the most powerful investigative disciplines‚Äîused by journalists, law enforcement, intelligence agencies, corporate security teams, private investigators, and curious individuals.

This playbook will teach you the techniques professionals use to find people, investigate companies, verify identities, and uncover hidden connections. Everything described here uses legal, publicly available sources‚Äîno hacking, no unauthorized access, just the systematic exploitation of information people have made public.

What OSINT Can Reveal

  • Identity verification ‚Äì Confirm who someone really is
  • Location history ‚Äì Track where someone has lived, worked, or traveled
  • Relationships ‚Äì Map connections between people and organizations
  • Financial indicators ‚Äì Identify assets, business interests, lawsuits
  • Background details ‚Äì Criminal records, education, employment history
  • Online activity ‚Äì Social media posts, forum comments, dating profiles
  • Real estate ‚Äì Properties owned, values, mortgages, sales history
  • Corporate structures ‚Äì Business ownership, shell companies, connections

The OSINT Mindset

Effective OSINT isn't about knowing every tool‚Äîit's about systematic thinking:

  1. Define your objective ‚Äì What specifically do you want to learn?
  2. Identify selectors ‚Äì What data points do you have to start? (name, email, phone, photo)
  3. Map the data landscape ‚Äì Where might relevant information exist?
  4. Collect systematically ‚Äì Document everything, preserve evidence
  5. Analyze connections ‚Äì Look for patterns, corroborate findings
  6. Verify and validate ‚Äì Never trust a single source

‚ö†Ô∏è Legal & Ethical Boundaries

OSINT uses only publicly available information. This guide does not cover hacking, unauthorized access, impersonation, or any illegal techniques. Even legal research can cross ethical lines‚Äîstalking, harassment, and doxing are never acceptable. Use these skills responsibly.

Chapter 5: People Search & Identification

Finding and identifying people is core to OSINT. This chapter covers techniques for locating individuals, verifying identities, and mapping their digital footprint.

Starting with a Name

  1. Google the name (with quotes) + location, employer, or other identifier
  2. Check people search sites ‚Äì FastPeopleSearch, ThatsThem, TruePeopleSearch
  3. Search social media ‚Äì Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram, Twitter
  4. Check voter records ‚Äì Many states publish voter files with addresses, DOB
  5. Property records ‚Äì County assessor sites show ownership
  6. Court records ‚Äì Civil and criminal cases

Starting with an Email Address

  1. Google the email ‚Äì May appear in leaked databases, forum posts, documents
  2. Check Have I Been Pwned ‚Äì Shows data breaches containing the email
  3. Email to social ‚Äì Epieos, Holehe check which platforms have accounts
  4. Domain whois ‚Äì If custom domain, check registration info
  5. Gravatar ‚Äì gravatar.com/[md5 hash of email] may show photo

üîß Email Investigation Tools

Starting with a Phone Number

  1. Reverse phone lookup ‚Äì TruePeopleSearch, ThatsThem, Whitepages
  2. Google the number ‚Äì May appear in ads, posts, business listings
  3. Check caller ID apps ‚Äì Truecaller, Hiya (may show saved name)
  4. Carrier lookup ‚Äì Identify carrier and number type (mobile/landline/VoIP)
  5. WhatsApp/Telegram ‚Äì Add number to see profile photo/name

Identity Verification Techniques

Once you find information, verify it's the right person:

Chapter 6: Business & Corporate Research

Corporate OSINT reveals company structures, ownership, financial health, and hidden connections. Whether investigating a business partner, competitor, or suspicious entity, these techniques expose what companies want hidden.

State Business Registrations

Every business entity (LLC, Corporation, etc.) must register with a state. These records reveal:

  • Date of formation
  • Registered agent (receives legal notices)
  • Officers and directors
  • Business address
  • Annual filings and status

SEC Filings (Public Companies)

Public companies must file detailed reports with the SEC. Key filings:

  • 10-K ‚Äì Annual report (comprehensive business overview)
  • 10-Q ‚Äì Quarterly financial reports
  • 8-K ‚Äì Material events (mergers, lawsuits, executive changes)
  • DEF 14A ‚Äì Proxy statement (executive compensation, shareholders)
  • Form 4 ‚Äì Insider trading reports
  • Schedule 13D/G ‚Äì Large shareholder disclosures

Search at: sec.gov/edgar/searchedgar/companysearch.html

Uncovering Shell Companies

Shell companies hide ownership. Red flags:

  • Registered agent services (CT Corporation, CSC, Registered Agent Inc.)
  • Formation in Delaware, Wyoming, Nevada (strong privacy laws)
  • No physical office or employees
  • Officers are other companies or nominees
  • Recent formation with large asset purchases

Piercing the Corporate Veil

  1. Check registered agent‚Äîsame agent = possibly same owner
  2. Search officer names across states
  3. Look for common addresses across entities
  4. Check UCC filings for financing relationships
  5. Search real property records for corporate ownership

Chapter 7: Property & Asset Discovery

Real property records are public in the United States, making property ownership one of the easiest assets to research. This chapter covers techniques for finding who owns what.

Real Property Records

County Assessor/Recorder Offices

Every county maintains property records showing:

  • Current owner name
  • Mailing address (sometimes different from property)
  • Assessed value and taxes
  • Property characteristics (size, beds/baths, year built)
  • Sales history and prices
  • Mortgage information

Free Property Search Sites

Finding Hidden Ownership

Properties may be held in LLCs, trusts, or corporate names. To find the real owner:

  1. Note the LLC/trust name on the deed
  2. Search state business records for LLC officers
  3. Check if the LLC's registered agent address matches other properties
  4. Search the trust name in court records (trusts often appear in probate)
  5. Look for financing documents‚Äîlenders often require personal guarantees

Vehicle Records

Vehicle ownership is harder to research due to DPPA restrictions, but some avenues exist:

  • VIN lookup ‚Äì NICB VinCheck (theft/total loss), NHTSA recalls
  • License plate lookup ‚Äì Limited legal options for non-law enforcement
  • Parking/traffic tickets ‚Äì Sometimes appear in court records
  • Social media ‚Äì People post their vehicles
  • Business vehicles ‚Äì DOT numbers, fleet registrations

Aircraft & Boats

Aircraft and boat registrations are more accessible:

UCC Filings

Uniform Commercial Code filings record security interests in personal property. When someone takes a loan secured by equipment, inventory, or accounts receivable, a UCC-1 is filed. These reveal:

  • Debtor name and address
  • Secured party (lender)
  • Collateral description
  • Filing date and status

Search at state SOS websites or aggregators like BizFilings.

Chapter 12: Geolocation Techniques

Geolocation‚Äîdetermining where a photo or video was taken‚Äîis one of OSINT's most powerful techniques. Combine visual clues with mapping tools to pinpoint locations.

Visual Clues to Look For

  • Signs and text ‚Äì Street signs, business names, license plates
  • Architecture ‚Äì Building styles vary by region
  • Vegetation ‚Äì Plants indicate climate/region
  • Sun position ‚Äì Indicates hemisphere and time
  • Shadows ‚Äì Help determine time of day
  • Infrastructure ‚Äì Power lines, road markings, vehicle types

Chapter 18: OPSEC: Protecting Yourself

When investigating, protect your own identity and digital footprint.

Browser Separation

  • Use separate browser profiles for research
  • Never log into personal accounts while researching
  • Consider a dedicated research VM

VPN & Anonymity

  • Use VPN for all research
  • Tor for sensitive investigations
  • Rotate exit nodes/servers

Sock Puppet Accounts

Create research personas:

  • Use burner email (ProtonMail, Tutanota)
  • VPN/Tor when creating
  • Consistent but fictional persona
  • Profile photos from ThisPersonDoesNotExist.com or properly licensed stock
  • Build history before using for research

‚ö†Ô∏è Account Policies

Creating fake accounts violates most platforms' terms of service. While common in OSINT, be aware of the risks and never use fake accounts for illegal purposes.

Physical Security

For sensitive investigations:

  • Don't research from identifiable locations
  • Be aware of who can see your screen
  • Use encrypted storage for research files
  • Consider the legal implications of your investigation

Scenario: ABC Consulting LLC solicits your business. Verify legitimacy.

  1. State business registration ‚Äì when formed, who are officers?
  2. WHOIS on their domain ‚Äì when registered, by whom?
  3. LinkedIn company page ‚Äì do employees look real?
  4. Google reviews, BBB, Glassdoor
  5. Reverse image search any photos
  6. Check if address is a virtual office
  7. Search officer names for other businesses, lawsuits

Case 3: Verifying a Social Media Profile

Scenario: Someone claims to be a doctor. Verify.

  1. Check medical license databases (state medical board)
  2. NPI (National Provider Identifier) registry
  3. Hospital/practice affiliation verification
  4. Medical school alumni directories
  5. Cross-reference photo with other sources
  6. Check for disciplinary actions
